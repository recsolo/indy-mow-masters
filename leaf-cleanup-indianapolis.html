Fall Leaf Removal in Indianapolis

Indianapolis fall foliage peaks from mid-October through early November, with leaves continuing to drop through December. The window for fall leaf removal is tight — wait too long and a thick layer of wet, matted leaves can smother your grass, block sunlight, and create the perfect breeding ground for lawn disease heading into winter.

A single heavy leaf layer left through winter can cause dead patches that take all spring to recover. We recommend at least one cleanup after peak leaf drop (late October to mid-November) and another sweep before the first hard freeze. Many Indianapolis homeowners also opt for a mid-October service to stay ahead of the heaviest accumulation. Whatever your timeline, we work around your schedule.

Frequently Asked Questions — Leaf Cleanup Indianapolis

When is the best time for leaf cleanup in Indianapolis?

Indianapolis leaf drop typically runs from mid-October through late November. We recommend at least one cleanup after the heaviest fall (late October to mid-November) and a final pass before the first hard freeze. Many homeowners do 2–3 visits throughout the season to protect their lawn.

Do you haul leaves away?

Yes. We can bag leaves and place them at the curb for Indianapolis city leaf pickup, or haul them off your property entirely. Just let us know your preference when requesting a quote — there's no extra fee for curb placement, and haul-away pricing is based on volume.

Can I schedule regular leaf cleanups throughout fall?

Absolutely — and we recommend it. Multiple visits prevent leaves from matting down and killing your grass. We offer flexible scheduling throughout October and November with no contracts required. Schedule as many or as few visits as your yard needs.
